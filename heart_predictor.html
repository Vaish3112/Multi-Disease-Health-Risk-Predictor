Heart Disease Risk Predictor

1. Age: Enter your age in years. Heart disease risk increases as you get older. For example, people over 45 (men) or 55 (women) have higher risk.


2. Gender: Select your gender. Risk levels may differ slightly between males and females.


3. Cholesterol: Total cholesterol level in mg/dL. High cholesterol can cause plaque build-up in arteries, increasing heart disease risk. You can measure it via a blood test at a lab or clinic.


4. Blood Pressure: Enter your systolic/diastolic pressure (e.g., 120/80 mmHg). High blood pressure forces the heart to work harder and is a major risk factor. You can measure it at home with a BP monitor or at a clinic.


5. Smoking: Select “Yes” if you smoke. Smoking damages blood vessels and greatly increases the risk of heart disease.


6. Diabetes: Select “Yes” if you have diabetes. Diabetes increases the risk of heart disease because high blood sugar can damage arteries over time.


7. Physical Activity Level: Choose your activity level. Regular exercise improves heart health and lowers risk. Examples:

  • Low: Mostly sitting, little or no exercise.
  • Medium: Walking or light exercise 2–3 times a week.
  • High: Regular exercise, sports, or physically active lifestyle.

8. Family History of Heart Disease: Select “Yes” if any close family members (parents or siblings) have had heart disease. Genetics can influence your risk.
